The ENIAC - 1943

The ENIAC started in 1943. The ENIAC was a type of computing system and it was built by John Mauchly and J. Presper Eckert . The ENIAC was built at the Moore School of Electrical Engineering of the University of Pennsylvania. It was about 1,000 times faster then any other computer in previous years. Besides being a computer it was also an Electronic Numerical Integrator. It was able to solve many different types of numerical problems. The ENIAC was very large it took up about 1,000 square feet and was 30 tons in weight. It also had panel to panel wiring and switching with about 18,000 vacuum tubes. It is said that is has probably done more calculations in its 10 years in operation in all of humanity until that time. The ENIAC is probably what helped the development of many other electronic developments like calculators, phone and more developed computers.
